DOUBLE RADIAL DIFFUSION (OUCHTERLONY TECHNIQUE)

 

REAGENT AND MATERIAL 

TEST PROCEDURE    

INTERPRETATION

  ANIMATION

   A highly useful technique in characterizing the complex relationships between antigens and antibodies is the “ double radial diffusion in gel systems ” developed by Ouchterlony in 1948. In this method, a layer of agar gel is made in a petridish or on the gel plate and circular wells are punched out at equal distances from one another in the gel. Antibody is then added to one well while antigen is added to the other. These materials are allowed to diffuse radially from their respective wells. A thin and sharp line of precipitation is formed at a zone where the antigen and antibody concentrations reach equivalence in the gel. The line is relatively straight and is perpendicular to the axis line between the two wells.

 

 

 

 

 


 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

REAGENTS AND MATERIALS        Back   

 ·        Microscope slides / Glass slides ( 3” X 1”  or  3” X 2”)

·        Capillaries

·        Antigen

·        Corresponding Antibodies

·        1% agar or agarose in phosphate buffer saline (PBS) (0.01 M,  pH 7.5) or in barbitone buffer (0.05 M,  pH 8.6) containing 0.01% sodium azide.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

TEST PROCEDURE                      Back   

·        Take 50 ml. Of  PBS or barbitone buffer in 100 ml. Conical flask.

·        Add 0.5 gm. Of agar or agarose to the buffer.

·        Dissolve the agar in boiling water bath or on low flame.

·        After the agar dissolves completely and solution is clear, filter the agar solution through cotton kept in funnel or through cheese cloth while it is hot.

 ·        Pour about 3 ml. Of hot clear agar solution on 3” X 1” microscopic slides or 6 ml. On 3” X 2” glass slides kept on leveled surface.

 ·        Wait for 10-15 minutes till the agar solidified.

 ·        Keep the slides in a humid chamber (e.g. petri dish with wet filter paper kept at bottom) at 40C for 20-30 minutes.

 ·        Punch out 2 mm wells with the help of punching set or with 2 mm bore steel tube (with smooth surface edge) attached to a suction pump. The inter-well distance can be 6-8 mm.

 ·        Fill the wells with antigen or antibody solution with the help of capillaries. Wait for 15-20 minutes.

 ·        Transfer the prepared slides to humid chamber. Keep it at room temperature overnight.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

INTERPRETATION                            Back                    

 REACTION OF IDENTITY

            If a solution of a pure antigen is placed in two wells and the the homologous antibody is placed in the other well, the two precipitin bands formed join at their contiguous ends.            

REACTION OF NON- IDENTITY

          If two unrelated antigens (having nothing in common) are placed in adjacent wells and diffuse towards a central well that contains antibodies for both two precipitin bands form independently of each other and cross.


   

 REACTION OF PARTIAL- IDENTITY

          If the antigen in one well shares some similarity to antigen in other well and when both the antigens diffuse towards a central well containing to the first antigen, the precipitation band will fuse (because of similarity in structure) and in addition form a spur that extends towards the cross reacting antigen (because of some unrelated structure).
