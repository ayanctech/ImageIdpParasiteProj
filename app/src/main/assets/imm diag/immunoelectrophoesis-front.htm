IMMUMOELECTROPHORESIS

 

REAGENT AND MATERIAL  TEST PROCEDURE   INTERPRETATION   ANIMATION

 

          Garbar and Williams (1953) first introduced the classical immunoelectrophoretic technique for detection of one or several antigens in a complex mixture. This technique represents a combination of physiochemical and immunochemical techniques.

            The method combines electrophoresis and free diffusion of antigens and antibodies resulting in the production of precipitin arcs corresponding to the various proteins contained in the specific antiserum used. The material to be examined is separated electrophoretically on agar gel and subsequently subjected to the effect of a precipitating antiserum. This is done by placing the antiserum in a trough in the agar parallel to the electrophoretically separated proteins. Both the antibodies and antigens diffuse towards one another forming upon confluence, well defined precipitin arcs in the form of more or less curved arcs.

 

 

 

 

 


 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

REAGENTS AND MATERIALS                                  

 

·        Amido black 0.5% in methanol : glacial acetic acid (9 :1)

·        Barbitone buffer (0.05 M, pH 8.6)

Sodium barbitone       10.3 g

Barbituric acid            1.84 g

1 litre distilled water

·        1% Agar in Barbitone buffer (0.05 M, pH 8.6)

·        Staining solution : 0.5 g amido black, 90.0 ml. Methanol and 10.0 ml. Acetic acid.

·        Destaining solution : 2% acetic acid.

·        Gel electrophoresis chamber with power pack.

·        Microscopic slides (75 mm or 25 mm), pre cleaned slides

·        Filter paper wicks

·        Whatman no. 3

·        Pasteur pipettes, microlitre pipettes.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

TEST PROCEDURE

 

 

·        Make 50 ml of 1% agar (W/V) solution in 0.05 M Barbitone buffer pH 8.0. A pinch of sodium azide is added as preservative.

 

·        Line up slides on leveling board and pour 3.2 ml hot agar solution on each slide witht the help of pipette. Wait for 20-30 minutes for the agar to solidify. Again keep for 15-20 minutes at 40C in humid chamber.

 

·        Punch out two wells (1.3 cm apart) (upper and lower in the middle of slides).

·        Apply antigen (2.5 ul) to the respective wells with the help of microsyringe.

 

·        Fill electrophoresis chamber with 0.05 M Barbitone buffer pH 8.6 on each side. Care should be taken to keep same level of buffer in both the chambers.

 

·        Place all the slides in the electrophoresis chamber and place Whatman no. 3 wet paper wicks (in buffer) over both the edge of the slides. Allow the other edge of the wicks to be in the buffer. Filter paper strips establish the contact between the agar on the slide and buffer in chambers.

 

·        Put 1 ul of bromophenol vlue dye marker solution in both the wells. Start electrophoresis at 80 volts (constant volts) till bromophenol blue marker reaches the end of the slide. It takes about 3 hours for the marker dye to reach at other end.

 

·        Disconnect the power supply, remove the slide and keep them on lavelling board. Cut the antisera trough 2 mm wide in the middle of two wells. Remove agar from the trough with the help of flattened 26 gauge needle.

 

·        Fill the trough with (0.04-0.06 ml) antisera with the help of pasture pipette or

 

·        Transfer the prepared slides to humid chamber. Keep it at room temperature overnight.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

INTERPRETATION                     

 

REACTION OF IDENTITY

            If a solution of a pure antigen is placed in two wells and the the homologous antibody is placed in the other well, the two precipitin bands formed join at their contiguous ends.

                       


                                   

REACTION OF NON- IDENTITY

          If two unrelated antigens (having nothing in common) are placed in adjacent wells and diffuse towards a central well that contains antibodies for both two precipitin bands form independently of each other and cross.


 

 

 


REACTION OF PARTIAL- IDENTITY

          If the antigen in one well shares some similarity to antigen in other well and when both the antigens diffuse towards a central well containing to the first antigen, the precipitation band will fuse (because of similarity in structure) and in addition form a spur that extends towards the cross reacting antigen (because of some unrelated structure).
