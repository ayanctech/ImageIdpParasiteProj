ROCKET ELECTROPHORESIS

 

REAGENT AND MATERIAL                 TEST PROCEDURE   

 

          Antigen is forced to diffuse (or moved) in one direction toeards positive pole in a gel containing antiserum, under the influence of electric current. Antigen reacts with antibody giving precipitin zones at equivalent point in the shape of peak or Rocket. The height of the rocket is directly propotional to the concentration of the antigen.

 

 

 

 

 


 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

REAGENTS AND MATERIALS                                  

 

·        Barbitone buffer (0.05 M, pH 8.0)

·        1% Agar in Barbitone buffer (0.05 M, pH 8.0)

·        Gel electrophoresis chamber with power pack.

·        Glass slides

·        Filter paper wicks

·        Whatman no. 3

·        Pasteur pipettes, microlitre pipettes.

·        Antigen

·        Corresponding antibody

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

TEST PROCEDURE  

·        Prepare 25 ml. Of clear solution of 1% agar or agarose and cool it to 50-55 0 C. mix 0.5 ml. Of antibody. After thorough mixing, 10 ml. Of mixture is poured on glass plate resting on an absolutely level surface. After short time at room temperature, the solution solidifies to a transparent gel of uniform thickness. The plates are ready for use and can be stored in a humid chamber at 40C.

 

·        Punch out wells 3 - 3.5 mm in diameter along the line parallel to one of the long edges of the plate leaving about 1.5 cm from the margin. The distance between adjac ent wells should be at least 5 mm. And should not be less than 15 mm from the edge.  

  

·        Fill wells with 2 ul of undiluted, 1:2 diluted, 1:4 or 1:8 diluted test antigen solution. Place moistened Whatman no. 3 paper strips in order to establish contact between buffer and gel plate.

 

·        Perform electrophoresis at 10 volts/cm immediately after    the test samples have been applied to the gel plate. Stop electrophoresis as soon as rocket   shape precipitin zones     are seen. Usually 1 – 3 hours are needed depending on the    electrophoretic mobility of the protein. The formation of immunoprecipitin peaks during electrophoresis can readily be observed with illumination from the slide, against  background. The process of electrophoresis is completed      when the tips of all the peaks (rocket) are closed.
